*   **Process:** This method uses an oxidizing agent to remove the methyl group.
*   **Reagents:**  Examples include:

    *   **Oxidation of Benzene:**  Over-oxidation of benzene to unwanted products (like maleic anhydride) is a potential issue.

**3.  Halogenation Followed by Reductive Elimination (Less Common, More Steps)**

*   **Process:** This is a multi-step approach.
    1. **Halogenation:**  React benzene with a halogen (e.g., chlorine, bromine) under free radical conditions (light or heat) to introduce a halogen atom onto the methyl group. This is difficult to control and often leads to multiple halogenations.
    2. **Reductive Elimination:**  The halogenated product is then treated with a reducing agent (e.g., zinc, magnesium) to remove the halogen and replace it with hydrogen, effectively removing the methyl group.
*   **Considerations:**
    *   **Multiple Steps:** This method is less efficient due to the multiple steps involved.
    *   **Control of Halogenation:**  Controlling the halogenation step to introduce only one halogen atom is difficult.
    *   **Waste Generation:**  This method can generate significant amounts of waste.
