RAG Answer:
Lie bumps come from transient lingual papillitis (TLP), which affects the fungiform papillae (taste buds) on the upper (dorsal) surface of the tongue. Possible causes include stress, gastrointestinal upset, menstruation, acidic or sour food, smoking, and local trauma.

**Common Causes of Bumps on the Tongue:**

Here's a breakdown, categorized by likelihood and severity. **Please read the important disclaimers at the very end of this response.**

**1. Most Common & Usually Harmless:**

*   **Papillae (Normal Variation):** Your tongue is covered in tiny bumps called papillae. These are normal! There are different types of papillae:
    *   **Filiform Papillae:** These are the most numerous and look like tiny, thread-like projections. They give the tongue its rough texture and help with grip.  Everyone has them, and their size and prominence can vary.  If they seem larger than usual, it could be due to irritation or mild swelling.
    *   **Fungiform Papillae:** These are small, mushroom-shaped bumps that contain taste buds. They are scattered across the tongue, especially towards the tip and sides.
    *   **Circumvallate Papillae:** These are larger, circular papillae located at the back of the tongue.  They are fewer in number.
*   **Transient Lingual Papillitis (Lie Bumps):** This is a very common condition where some of the fungiform papillae become inflamed and painful. They appear as red, swollen bumps. The cause isn't always clear, but it's often linked to:
    *   Stress
    *   Dietary factors (acidic foods, spicy foods)
    *   Minor trauma (biting your tongue)
    *   Allergies
    *   Hormonal changes
    *   These bumps usually resolve on their own within a few days to a couple of weeks.
*   **Irritation:**  Things like smoking, alcohol, mouthwashes containing alcohol, or even certain toothpastes can irritate the tongue and cause bumps.

**2. Less Common, but Possible:**

*   **Oral Thrush (Candidiasis):** This is a fungal infection that can cause white or yellowish patches on the tongue, which may feel like bumps. It's more common in people with weakened immune systems, diabetes, or those taking antibiotics.
*   **Aphthous Ulcers (Canker Sores):** These are small, painful sores that can appear on the tongue or inside the mouth. They aren't contagious.
*   **Viral Infections:**  Certain viral infections, like herpes simplex virus (HSV), can cause sores or bumps on the tongue.
*   **Allergies:**  Allergies to foods, medications, or other substances can sometimes manifest as bumps on the tongue.
*   **Geographic Tongue:** This condition causes patches of smooth, red areas on the tongue that resemble a map. The appearance can change over time. It's generally harmless.
*   **Lichen Planus:** This is an inflammatory condition that can affect the mouth, including the tongue. It can cause white, lacy patches or red, swollen areas.

**3. Rare, but Important to Rule Out:**

*   **Oral Cancer:** While rare, persistent bumps or sores on the tongue that don't heal within a few weeks should be evaluated by a doctor or dentist.  Other signs of oral cancer can include a lump, thickening, or difficulty swallowing.
