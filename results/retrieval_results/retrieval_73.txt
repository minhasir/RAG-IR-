Okay, let's break down the partial pressure of carbon dioxide in arterial blood.

The approximate partial pressure of carbon dioxide (PCO2) in arterial blood is **40 mmHg (millimeters of mercury)**.

Here's a little more context:

*   **Normal Range:** The normal range is generally considered to be 35-45 mmHg.
*   **What it represents:** PCO2 reflects the amount of carbon dioxide dissolved in the blood. It's a key indicator of how well carbon dioxide is being transported from the tissues to the lungs for exhalation.
*   **Clinical Significance:** Deviations from this range can indicate respiratory problems (like hypoventilation or hyperventilation) or metabolic issues.
