Question: what is the valley of dry bones about

Reference Answer: In his vision the prophet sees himself standing in the valley full of dry human bones . He is commanded to carry a prophecy . Before him the bones connect into human figures , then the bones become covered with tendon tissues , flesh and skin . Then God reveals the bones to the prophet as the People of Israel in exile and commands the Prophet to carry another prophecy in order to revitalize these human figures , to resurrect them and to bring them to the Land of Israel .

Okay, let's unpack the "Valley of Dry Bones" – a powerful and symbolic vision from the book of Ezekiel (Ezekiel 37). Here's a breakdown of what it's about, its layers of meaning, and its significance:

**1. The Literal Scene (Ezekiel 37:1-14):**

*   **The Vision:** Ezekiel is led by the Spirit to a valley filled with dry bones. They are scattered across the ground, bleached white, and utterly lifeless.
*   **God's Command:** God asks Ezekiel a rhetorical question: "Can these bones live?" (This is to highlight the hopelessness of the situation). Then, God commands Ezekiel to propheshesize over the bones.
*   **The Prophecy:** Ezekiel obeys and propheshes, speaking God's words: "Hear the word of the Lord… I will cause breath to enter you, and you shall live."
*   **The Result:** As Ezekiel prophesies, a rattling sound begins. The bones start to come together, sinew and flesh cover them, and skin covers them. But they are still lifeless. Then, God commands the Spirit to breathe into them, and they come to life! A vast army fills the valley.

**2. Levels of Interpretation (What does it *mean*?):**

The Valley of Dry Bones is rich in symbolism and has been interpreted on multiple levels throughout history. Here are the main ones:

*   **Immediate Historical Context (Israel's Exile):** The most immediate interpretation relates to the Israelites who had been exiled to Babylon. They were scattered, seemingly destroyed as a nation, and felt hopeless. The vision represents God's promise to regather them, restore their land, and rebuild their nation. It's a message of hope and restoration for a people who felt abandoned.  The "dry bones" represent the dead and scattered nation of Israel.
*   **Spiritual Resurrection (Individual Salvation):**  Beyond the national level, the vision speaks to the spiritual condition of individuals.  "Dry bones" can represent those who are spiritually dead – alienated from God, lacking faith, or living in sin. Ezekiel's prophecy and the breath of God symbolize the power of God to bring people back to life spiritually through repentance and faith. It's a picture of conversion and regeneration.
*   **The Church (The Body of Christ):**  Many Christians interpret the vision as a representation of the Church.  The "dry bones" can symbolize a church that has become stagnant, lifeless, or divided. God's breath represents the Holy Spirit, who is needed to revitalize and empower the church to fulfill its mission.
*   **End Times Restoration:** Some interpretations see the vision as a prophecy about the end times, when God will restore all things and bring about a final, glorious kingdom.

**3. Key Themes and Significance:**

*   **God's Power to Restore:** The central theme is God's incredible power to bring life out of death, hope out of despair, and restoration out of destruction. It demonstrates that nothing is impossible for God.
*   **The Importance of Prophecy/Speaking God's Word:** Ezekiel's obedience to prophesy, even when the situation seemed hopeless, was crucial to the restoration. It highlights the power of God's word to bring about change.
*   **The Role of the Holy Spirit:** The "breath" of God is explicitly identified as the Spirit, emphasizing the Spirit's essential role in bringing about spiritual life and renewal.
*   **Hope in the Face of Hopelessness:** The vision offers a powerful message of hope for those who are facing difficult circumstances, feeling lost, or believing that their situation is beyond repair.

**In essence, the Valley of Dry Bones is a powerful illustration of God's unwavering commitment to restoration, renewal, and the bringing of life where there was once only death.**
